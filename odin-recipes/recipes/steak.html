Steak

Description

Steak frites, a beloved classic in French cuisine, is a dish celebrated for its simplicity and robust flavors. At its core, steak frites consists of two main components: a perfectly cooked steak and a side of golden, crispy French fries. The steak, often a cut like sirloin or ribeye, is seasoned generously, seared to a mouthwatering medium-rare or medium doneness, and then allowed to rest, allowing the juices to redistribute for optimal tenderness.

The accompanying frites, or fries, are a crucial element, providing a satisfying contrast in texture. These fries are typically cut into slender, uniform strips, then twice-cooked to achieve a crispy exterior and a fluffy interior. The combination of the succulent, flavorful steak and the crispy, golden fries creates a delightful harmony on the plate. Often served with a side of aioli or a classic BÃ©arnaise sauce for dipping, steak frites embodies the elegance of French bistro cuisine while offering a hearty and satisfying dining experience.
