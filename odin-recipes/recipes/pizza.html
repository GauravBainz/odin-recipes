Pizza

Description

A New York-style pizza is characterized by its thin, foldable crust that strikes a perfect balance between crispness and chewiness. The crust is thin enough to be easily folded, yet substantial enough to hold a generous layer of classic tomato sauce and whole-milk mozzarella cheese. This iconic pizza style is often adorned with a variety of toppings, from the traditional pepperoni and sausage to mushrooms and bell peppers, creating a delightful medley of flavors.

The hallmark of a New York-style pizza lies in its simplicity and quality ingredients. The emphasis on a straightforward yet well-executed combination of crust, sauce, cheese, and toppings has made this pizza a beloved classic, especially in the bustling streets of New York City. The result is a delicious, satisfying slice that captures the essence of one of the world's most iconic pizza styles.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • 1 Teasppon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cups warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Tomato sauce
  • Cheese
  • Your choice of toppings

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough
  2. Preheat the oven
  3. Roll out the dough
  4. Prepare the toppings
  5. Bake the pizza
  6. Cool and slice
  7. Enjoy!
